# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Busy parents — primarily mums — with young children in Glasgow and East Renfrewshire. Often overwhelmed by the mental load of new parenthood. They're time-poor, standards-conscious, and relieved to find someone who will handle this one thing completely. Secondary users: grandparents, childminders, nurseries, and parents preparing pre-loved gear for resale. Context: mobile-first discovery, often in moments of low attention. The decision to book is partly emotional (relief, trust) and partly practical (price, turnaround time).

## Product Purpose

Squeaky Clean Strollers is a professional baby equipment cleaning service run by Charlotte Kerr, based in East Renfrewshire. The website exists to convert visitors into bookings. Charlotte cleans buggies, car seats, travel cots, high chairs and all baby gear using baby-safe, non-toxic products. Success means a customer lands on the site, immediately understands the value, trusts Charlotte, and books (or at minimum makes contact).

## Brand Personality

Premium, warm, trustworthy. A specialist who takes this seriously — not a hobbyist with a bucket. The tone is confident but never corporate: Charlotte built this from personal experience and the brand should feel like it. Emotionally: the site should make parents feel seen, relieved, and certain they've found the right person.

## Anti-references

- **kiddikleen.com** — the main local competitor. Looks cheap, low-effort, dated. The design of this site is a direct reason to look different.
- **Generic Wix / Squarespace small business templates** — cookie-cutter layouts, stock-photo feel, low typographic ambition. The target aesthetic is the opposite: editorial, considered, premium.
- **Busy / cluttered baby product sites** — loud colours, excessive iconography, cartoon-adjacent design. This site should feel grown-up and calm.

## Design Principles

1. **Premium by restraint** — whitespace, typography and photography do the heavy lifting. Decoration is earned, not default.
2. **Trust before conversion** — Charlotte's story and real reviews earn the booking. The site never pushes before it persuades.
3. **One less thing to worry about** — every design decision should reduce friction and anxiety, not add it. Clear pricing, simple booking, no hidden steps.
4. **Personal, not corporate** — the brand is Charlotte. The site should feel like it was made for her specifically, not templated for a category.
5. **Local credibility** — East Renfrewshire and Glasgow are specific, real signals of trust for the target audience. The site should reinforce this clearly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. All images have descriptive alt text. Booking flow must be keyboard-navigable.
